EVRI Case Study


This was a short limited case study to demonstrate my understanding of emotional design and AI acceptance (UX principles?) on a suitable mobile application

-----------------------------------

Overview

Improving EVRI's Customer Service Chatbot

Role: UX Researcher and Designer

EVRI recieves many negative reviews revealing a common theme of issues with EVRI’s chatbot and/or their customer service in general. This project evaluated the usability of the EVRI customer service chatbot including ways to improve the user experience through trust, efficiency and emotional connection.

1

Problem

Customer reviews and industry reports show user frustrations with EVRI’s customer support. Many users rely on the chatbot service for issues with deliveries, but a lack of understanding was leading to hightened frustration revealing low usability and task completion.

Key Concerns:

  • Lack of trust in AI responses
  • Chatbot misinterpreting user questions
  • Issues finding the chatbot from the app's homepage
  • Missing human connection often associated with good customer service
2

Research

I created a user persona and user journey...

I conducted usability testing with each participant completing the same three tasks:

  1. Navigate from the homepage to the chatbot and track a parcel
  2. Open a new chat discussing a missing parcel
  3. Ask the chatbot for collection advice

The participants were encouraged to 'Think Aloud' while completing the tasks to reveal further usability issues.

The chatbot was further evaluated using the BUS-15 Chatbot Usability Scale

Overall Usability Score: 2.5/5

3

Key Insights

Testing revealed three major issues:

  1. Poor Chatbot discoverability
    100% of participants, new to the EVRI app, struggled to find the chatbot from the app homepage

  2. Lack of understanding
    The chatbot frequently struggled to interpret slight differences in prompts and commonly misinterpreted or ignored user queries as a result. Although the responses were clear and timely there is a lack of understanding for vague or complex prompts.

    Feedback from participants:

    “When asked the question the chatbot response seemed like there was no understanding.”

    “I feel the chat was very inefficient when it came to new prompts and seemed to only work when the options were clicked and the text entered had no errors"

  3. Negative User Experience
    100% of participants were either confused or frustrated starting a new chat
4

Solution

Improvement 1 - Clear entry point

  • 'Help' poster for homepage
  • Re-design for chatbot pop-up

Improvement 2 - Improving clarity of 'End chat' button

  • New 'End Chat' button
  • New 'End Chat' pop-up

Improvement 3 - Implementing error handling

  • Allow for user input errors
  • Embracing chatbot misunderstanding

Improvement 4 - Humanised chatbot

I created a new visual identity for Ezra to facilitate a more 'human-like' connection and promote an empathetic reaction

5

Impact and Limitations

These changes aim to:

  • Increase trust in EVRI’s customer service
  • Improve chatbot comprehension and usability
  • Reduce customer frustrations throughout support channels

Limitations

  • Low sample size and variation
  • Short testing timeframe
  • Participant bias (all napier students)
  • No access to the backend
