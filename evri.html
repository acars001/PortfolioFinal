EVRI AI Chatbot Usability Study

This project evaluated the usability of EVRI's customer service chatbot to identify key user frustrations and produce design improvements with an aim to increase trust, efficiency and emotional engagement.

Utilising usability testing and qualitative feedback, I explored how users interacted with the chatbot to create targeted design changes to improve the support experience.

Aim: Improve EVRI's Customer Service Chatbot

Role: UX Researcher and Designer

Problem

Customer reviews and industry reports highlight frequent user frustration with EVRI’s customer support. Many customers rely on the chatbot service for issues with deliveries, but a lack of understanding was leading to hightened frustration and revealed low usability and task completion.

Support tools and general customer service are often used during times of heightened stress for customers (e.g when parcels have been misdelivered), system limitations can then lead to a significant increase in user frustration, emphasising the importace of chatbot usability



Key Concerns:

  • Lack of trust in AI responses
  • Chatbot misinterpreting user questions
  • Issues finding the chatbot from the app's homepage
  • Missing human connection often associated with good customer service

Research

Initially a user group was selected and then a user journey was made to visualise customer behaviour, needs and pain points.


A Usability study was conducted with participants who were aged 18-40 and who regularly shopped online. Each participant completed the same three tasks:

  1. Navigate from the homepage to the chatbot and track a parcel
  2. Open a new chat discussing a missing parcel
  3. Ask the chatbot for collection advice

The participants were encouraged to 'Think Aloud' while completing the tasks to reveal further usability issues.

The chatbot was further evaluated using the BUS-15 Chatbot Usability Scale

Overall Usability Score: 2.5/5

Key Insights

Testing revealed three major issues:

  1. Poor Chatbot discoverability
    100% of participants, new to the EVRI app, struggled to find the chatbot from the app homepage. This suggests the chatbot discoverability is low and may not align with users' expectations.

  2. Negative User Experience
    100% of participants were either confused or frustrated starting a new chat. This seemed to be as a result of there being multiple ways to perform similar tasks that had different outcomes.

  3. Lack of understanding
    The chatbot frequently struggled to interpret slight differences in prompts and commonly misinterpreted or ignored user queries as a result. Although the responses were clear and timely there is a lack of understanding for vague or complex prompts.

    Feedback from participants:

“When asked the question the chatbot response seemed like there was no understanding.”

“I feel the chat was very inefficient when it came to new prompts and seemed to only work when the options were clicked and the text entered had no errors"

Solution

1 - Clear entry point

To improve discoverability, a clearer entry poster was introduced to the homepage, in addition to the redesigned chatbot pop-up. This allows users to quickly access support when necessary.

  • 'Help' poster for homepage
  • Re-design for chatbot pop-up

2 - Improving clarity of 'End chat' button

To avoid user error and confusion I created one path for users to end/create new chat.

  • New 'End Chat' button
  • New 'End Chat' pop-up

3 - Implementing error handling

The chatbot failed to understand any user input error or interpret complex prompts. This led to a feeling of one-sided conversation that was fixed by allowing users to alter entries and let the chatbot know if it wasn't helping to solve the original problem.

  • Allow for user input errors
  • Embracing chatbot misunderstanding

4 - Humanised chatbot

To address the lack of emotional engagement creating a disconnect between users and service, I designed a new visual identity for the chatbot. Giving Ezra, the chatbot name, a distinguishable character helps to create a more humanised interaction and can encourage users to feel more at ease when interacting with automated support. This is increasingly important with the rise of AI and hesitant AI acceptance

Summary

Users couldn't find the chatbot -> Designed a clearer homepage entry

Users confused ending chats -> Created clear 'End Chat' pathway

Chatbot misunderstanding prompts -> Added user and chatbot error-handling

Users couldn't connect with the chatbot -> Utilised empathetic design for Ezra identity

Impact and Limitations

These changes aim to:

  • Increase trust in EVRI’s customer service by reducing customer frustrations
  • Improve chatbot comprehension and usability by improving chatbot responses to user errors and reducing confusion with ending conversations
  • Improve chatbot discoverability from the homepage
  • Create a more empathetic and communicative chatbot experience

Limitations

  • Small sample size and variation
  • Short testing timeframe
  • No access to the backend

Reflection

  • Small design changes can be just as effective as a full redesign, resulting in a more targeted and focused approach
  • Emotional design is of high priority in a customer service context
  • User testing revealed issues that were not initially clear during initial analysis
